# The Glass Road

No one remembers who built it, or how. The Glass Road stretches from the western seas to the eastern wastes, a perfectly smooth ribbon of pale green glass that does not crack, does not weather, does not heat under the sun.

It reflects the sky above it, but not always the sky of this world. Travelers report seeing distant cities, unfamiliar constellations, scenes from histories that never happened.

The Road Walkers, an ancient guild of pilgrim cartographers, walk it from end to end as a rite of passage. Those who complete the journey return changed. Those who do not, vanish.

But what if the reflections are not other places, but other versions of those who walk?
