# The Ember Reef

Far below the ocean's surface, where light should not reach, the Ember Reef glows with a fire that water cannot extinguish. Coral branches pulse with warm orange light, and small fish carry single flames within their translucent bodies.

The Tideweavers, an ancient order of underwater scholars, study the reef from coral cathedrals built around its perimeter. They claim the embers were lit by something that fell from the sky long ago.

The currents around the reef move strangely, weaving the heat into shapes that no one can quite remember after leaving.

But what if the embers were never fire at all?
