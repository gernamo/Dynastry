# Signal Lost

The irony is that nothing was lost. That was the problem.

On this night the 23:40's ancient router held. No four-minute windows, no six-minute deaths. Just a clean, flat, uninterrupted signal from the central station all the way to the coast. They could type as much as they wanted, as fast as they wanted, with no pause to make a word count.

So they did. And it was fine. It was pleasant. It was a perfectly nice conversation between two strangers on a train, the kind you have and forget by the next morning. *where are you headed. cool. yeah I needed to get away too. ha.* The messages came easy and meant nothing, because everything that's easy and means nothing comes easy and means nothing.

There were no held breaths. No six minutes of watching dark fields and rehearsing the brave thing. No pressure cooker turning two scared people into something urgent. The constraint had been the whole engine, and without it the machine just idled. They never told each other the real things. Why bother? There was always more time, more signal, another message. Infinite access made every individual message worthless.

She got to her stop. *nice talking to you,* she typed, and meant it exactly as much as it sounds. *you too,* he sent back, instantly, because the signal was perfect. She got off. The chat, as always, didn't follow her off the train. She didn't notice. There was nothing to follow.

Somewhere a router worked perfectly, and because of it, nothing happened. The near-miss they never knew they missed. Last Connection is a love story that only exists in the gaps — and this is the branch that proves it, by removing them.
